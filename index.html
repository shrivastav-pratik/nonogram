How to play Nonogram

A nonogram is a picture logic puzzle. The numbers tell you how many cells to fill — your job is to work out which ones, using logic alone. Every puzzle here has exactly one solution and never needs guessing.

Reading the clues

Each number on a row or column is the length of a run of filled cells, listed in order. Runs are separated by at least one empty cell.

Example — a row clue 3 1 means a block of 3 filled cells, then a gap, then 1 filled cell:

3 1

A clue of 0 means that row or column is completely empty.

Controls

  • Tap / left-click a cell to fill it (). Tap again to clear it.
  • Tap the Mode button to switch to Mark ✕, then tap cells you've ruled out to flag them with an X. (On a computer, right-click also marks.)
  • Drag across cells to paint several at once.
  • Undo reverses your last action.

Buttons

  • New — generate a fresh puzzle at the chosen size.
  • Check — tells you how many filled cells are wrong (without revealing the answer).
  • Clear — empties the whole grid.
  • 🌙 / ☀️ — switch between dark and light themes.

Winning

A row or column clue turns green when it's satisfied. Fill every cell correctly and the puzzle solves itself — your X marks don't matter, only the filled cells.
